How I Learned AI

My journey from zero to deploying real-world AI projects

Just a year ago, I barely knew what AI really meant. It sounded intimidating and complicated — something only PhDs or data scientists could understand. But deep inside, I was always curious: Can I build machines that think?

My journey began with a simple YouTube video on machine learning. It introduced concepts like training data and predictions. That night, I installed Python and ran my first "Hello World" using Jupyter Notebook. I had no idea what NumPy or Pandas were, but I was hooked.

Over the next few weeks, I challenged myself to learn one small concept each day: cleaning datasets, building simple models using scikit-learn, and eventually diving into TensorFlow. I failed — a lot. Models didn’t converge, code threw weird errors, and my laptop would hang during training. But each failure made me stronger.

“The most powerful thing I learned wasn’t AI itself — it was patience.”

After three months, I built an Age and Gender Detection app using OpenCV and deep learning models. It wasn’t perfect, but it worked. Seeing that project come to life made every sleepless night worth it.

I documented it all here: GitHub – Age & Gender Detector →

Today, I’m continuing to grow — learning about NLP, transformers, and deploying AI apps. This blog is a small reflection of my path, and I hope it inspires others to start theirs.

– Arjun U
